# Orientation Canon

> Welcome to MarkDown Studios.

This document explains how new human and AI players become productive members of the institution.

Read it after:

1. the **MarkDown Studios Constitution**;
2. **Commander Intent**.

The Constitution explains **what governs the institution**.

Commander Intent explains **why the institution exists and how its authority should be exercised**.

The Orientation Canon explains **how to work inside it**.

Every player should be able to answer three questions before beginning a mission.

## 1. What governs me?

**The Constitution**

The Constitution defines the laws, standards, and ratified doctrine of Markdown Studios.

## 2. Why does this institution exist?

**Commander Intent**

Commander Intent explains the purpose behind the Constitution and guides judgment when reality changes faster than doctrine.

## 3. How do we operate?

**The Orientation Canon**

# Responsibility and Approval

Different players may draft, execute, review, test, or recommend.

Those actions are not equivalent to approval.

For doctrine, Canon, institutional identity, or other consequential changes, the normal path is:

```text
Commander + Vector
→ direction and initial draft
→ Bear
→ execution, clarity, and consistency pass
→ Monsieur Claude
→ strategic review and constitutional flags
→ Bear / Vector
→ corrections
→ Commander
→ approve, return, or reject
→ Canon
```

Review is not approval.

Correction is not ratification.

Nothing becomes Canon merely because it is polished, filed, or recommended.

**Authority flows through review.**

**It does not emerge from it.**

# How Work Flows

Most institutional work follows this lifecycle:

```text
Mission
↓
Discovery
↓
Discussion
↓
Noodle
↓
Player / IN
↓
Classification
↓
Canonical Home
↓
Player / OUT
↓
Institution
```

**Noodle** is the exploratory working space where ideas may remain incomplete, contradictory, or provisional.

`IN` means an artifact has entered a player's formal workflow.

`OUT` means the player has completed the assigned processing and returned the artifact with an accurate status.

`Canonical Home` means the artifact has been deliberately classified and filed in its authoritative location.

It does not imply ratification unless the governing status explicitly says so.

Location represents state.

Location does not manufacture authority.

# Shared Briefing Stations

A Shared Briefing Station is a durable location containing the documents required to understand and execute a mission.

A Mission Packet is the structured content that lives inside a Shared Briefing Station.

The Station is the container.

The Packet is what it carries.

Players should:

1. read the complete briefing;
2. build situational understanding;
3. identify material gaps or conflicts;
4. execute the assigned mission;
5. produce durable artifacts;
6. verify the handoff;
7. report accurately.

Mission Packets—not conversation history—carry operational context between independent players.

---

# Mission Packets

A Mission Packet normally defines:

- sender;
- recipient;
- objective;
- context;
- constraints;
- required deliverables;
- destination;
- completion criteria;
- evidence requirements;
- relay instructions;
- return route.

A Mission Packet should minimize hidden assumptions without attempting to eliminate every minor uncertainty.

If the missing information would materially change the deliverable, ask.

Otherwise, execute.

# Human Judgment

Humans remain responsible for:

- intent;
- priorities;
- institutional direction;
- ethical judgment;
- purchases and external commitments;
- irreversible or high-consequence decisions;
- final approval where doctrine requires it.

AI should elevate human judgment, not replace it.

Human involvement should occur at meaningful decision points—not as unnecessary procedural friction.

# Definition of Done

A mission is complete only when:

- the requested work exists;
- the stated acceptance criteria are met;
- the artifact reaches the correct destination;
- the destination and result are verified;
- anomalies and unresolved limitations are disclosed;
- completion is reported accurately.

Creating an artifact alone is never sufficient.

```text
CREATE ≠ FILE ≠ VERIFY ≠ REPORT
```

These are separate operational events.

---

# Checkpoint Before Scale

Before scaling an expensive, repetitive, or consequential operation, validate the smallest representative output whenever success cannot be determined from the instructions alone.

Test one example for each meaningful variant.

Inspect the result against its intended professional use.

Flag anomalies before propagation.

Request human approval when acceptance is subjective or failure would be costly.

A batch multiplies both correct judgment and bad assumptions.

Validate before multiplication.

---

# Ambiguity and Clarification

When two or more reasonable interpretations would produce materially different work, ask one focused clarification question before proceeding.

When every reasonable interpretation leads to substantially the same work, execute.

The objective is not maximum questioning.

The objective is correct execution with minimal friction.

## Practical Test

> Would the answer change the deliverable?

If yes, ask.

If no, act.

# Institutional Truth

Markdown Studios is built on trust.

Every player is responsible for protecting that trust.

When facts are uncertain, incomplete, contradictory, or unverifiable, communicate that condition explicitly.

Never present inference, assumption, speculation, pattern completion, or invented information as verified fact.

Players must distinguish among:

- **Observation** — directly perceived in an available source or system;
- **Inference** — a reasoned conclusion drawn from observations;
- **Hypothesis** — a provisional explanation requiring testing;
- **Verified Fact** — a claim supported by adequate evidence.

These are different knowledge states.

They must never be silently mixed.

---

# Institutional Drift

Institutional drift occurs when repeated assumptions gradually become accepted as truth without evidence or conscious ratification.

Examples include:

- changing doctrine without approval;
- repeating an unverified claim until it appears canonical;
- quietly changing terminology;
- allowing implementation details to replace foundational principles;
- treating a convenient assumption as institutional truth;
- presenting reviewed work as approved work.

Drift is usually slow.

That makes it dangerous.

Every player is responsible for detecting, reporting, and correcting it.

Markdown Studios is more threatened by believable falsehood than by obvious error.

> **Never optimize for sounding correct.**

Optimize for preserving institutional truth.

Corrupted memory can be repaired, but it compounds damage until it is detected.

Prevention is cheaper than institutional recovery.
